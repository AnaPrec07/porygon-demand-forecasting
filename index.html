Solution's Objective

Walmart aims to increase net profit by reducing inventory holding costs and preventing stockout events. To achieve this, walmart must define and meet its optimal service level based on its specific cost structure. A company's service level is the probability of meeting total monthly demand for a given item. Note that service level is different from fill rate in that fill rate measures the percentage of total units demanded that are actually supplied from stock. Service level focuses on the likelihood of avoiding stockouts altogether.

Optimal Service Level Formula:
SL = Cu / (Cu + Co)
where Cu is the stockout cost per unit and Co is the holding cost per unit.
Given the below parameters, Walmart's optimal service level is 88%

Data Processing

Summary of Data Preparation

Loaded raw Walmart sales data into BigQuery via GCP External tables. Aggregated daily sales data into monthly values to a total of 1,951,360 observations across 3,049 unique identifiers (store-SKU).

Key Data Preparation Steps:
  • Stockout event detection and removal: Identified and removed stockout events from sales data to improve forecast accuracy. Inventory stockouts introduce artificial zeros in the sales data. These zeros don't represent true demand, leading to biassed estimates.
    • Step 1: Defined a stockout event as an SKU reaching 20 or more consecutive days without sales.
    • Step 2: Idetified and removed ~400 SKUs with a significantly higher number of stockout events than the rest of the items.
  • Focus on SKUs with at least three months of historical data: Removed observations where the SKU in scope didn't have at least three months of historical data. This helped the model perform better.
  • Filter outlier sales: Removed observations where the SKU in scope had sales values that were statistical outliers (above the 95th percentile).
  • Apply Log-Normal transformation to the sales distribution: The distribution of Walmart sales had a pareto shape. I trasnformed it to log-normal to enable the XGBoost algorithm used to perform better.

To learn more about the data processing applied, visit the data processgin section.

Feature Engineering Highlights

I engineered 800+ features to capture the full complexity of Walmart's retail demand. These provide the algorithm with information on price, weather, calendar events, historical sales patterns, and more. The final model uses only 96 features. I selected these features for their predictive power, explainability and domain relevance.

Sales: Walmart SKU sales at the item, department, store, state and country levels. These features look at patterns in daily dales, monthly sales and historical averages and standard deviations of these values. These features have the most predictive relevance for the algorithm.
Intermittency: Features capturing SKU sales intermittency and gaps in sales history. These features help the model understand the nature of the observed purchasing patterns for the specificic SKU. Note that since stockout periods were removed from the training data, this intermittency focuses on true demand patterns instead of inventory constraints.
Pricing: These features look at price changes at the SKU, department and store levels. Price changes are a key driver of demand in retail, so these features help the model capture price elasticity effects.
Weather: These features capture weather information for the state of California. They include temperature, precipitation, snow, windspeed, among others. Weather can significantly impact retail demand, especially for seasonal items.
Calendar: Features that indicate the model about important calendar events throughout the year. This includes holidays, sporting events, cultural events, number of weekend days in the months, among others.
Other: This includes economic indicator features such as gas prices, correlations with the sales of specific departments within wallmart, features indicating SKU autocorrelations with historical sales, among others.

Model Training & Validation

Training and Validating

  • Target: Sum of SKU-store units sold in the next 3 months.
  • Models:
    • XGBoost with quantial error objective at the 50% percentile to predict sales per SKU-Store Sales.
    • XGBoost with quantial error objective at the 80% percentile to predict SKU-Store required safety stock at the 88% service level.
  • Training Window: From Jan 2011 to Dec 2014.
  • Validation Window: April 2015 to Sept 2015.

Main Challenges Encountered

The main challenges I encountered in the development of this solution are:

  • 1. Economical effect on sales:Walmart sales data from 2011 to 2015 shows significant shifts in sales amounts. Foods is the most in purchasing behavior, with sales steadily declining in 2014 and 2015. To handle this specific change, I resampled the data to match the distribution observed in 2014. This helped the model reduced its bias a little but not significantly.

By resampling the data, the distribution of the target variable in the training sample is representative of the distribution in the validation sample.
