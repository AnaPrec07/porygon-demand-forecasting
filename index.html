Solution's Objective

Walmart aims to increase net profit by reducing inventory holding costs and preventing stockout events. To achieve this, walmart must define and meet its optimal service level. A company's service level is the probability of meeting total monthly demand for a given item.

Optimal Service Level Formula:
SL = Cu / (Cu + Co)
where Cu is the stockout cost per unit and Co is the holding cost per unit.
Given the below parameters, Walmart's optimal service level is 78%

Data Processing

Summary of Data Preparation

Loaded raw Walmart sales data into BigQuery via GCP External tables. Aggregated daily sales data into monthly values to a total of 1,951,360 observations across 3,049 unique identifiers (store-SKU).

Key Data Preparation Steps:
  • Stockout event detection and removal: Identified and removed stockout events from sales data to improve forecast accuracy. Inventory stockouts introduce artificial zeros in the sales data. These zeros don't represent true demand, leading to biassed estimates.
  • Focus on SKUs with at least three months of historical data: Removed observations where the SKU in scope didn't have at least three months of historical data to improve forecast accuracy.
  • Filter outlier sales: Removed observations where the SKU in scope had monthly sales above the 95th percentile.
  • Apply Log-Normal transformation to the sales distribution: The distribution sales has a pareto shape. I trasnformed it to log-normal to enable the XGBoost algorithm to perform better.

Model Details

Training Parameters

  • Target: Sum of SKU-store units sold in the next 3 months.
  • Models: The solution uses two models. One for accuracy and one for safety stock predictions.
    • XGBoost with quantial error objective at the 50% percentile to predict sales per SKU-Store Sales.
    • XGBoost with quantial error objective at the 80% percentile to predict SKU-Store required safety stock at the 88% service level.
  • Training Window: From Jan 2011 to Dec 2014.
  • Validation Window: April 2015 to Dec 2015.

Feature Engineering

I engineered 800+ features to capture the full complexity of Walmart's retail demand. These provide the algorithm with information on price, weather, calendar events, historical sales patterns, and more. The final model uses only 93 features. I selected these features for their predictive power and stability over time.
