Data Collection & Exploration

Overview

This section describes the process of collecting and exploring the Walmart datasets available from the M5 case competition.

Data Sources

  • Kaggle M5 Case Competition Datasets:Information on item sales, prices, and calendar events.Learn more.
  • BigQuery Public Datasets:California weather data, such as temperature and precipitation.Learn more.
  • US Bureau of Labor Statistics:Economic indicators such as gas prices, CPI, and unemployment.Learn more.

Data Collection

  • Ingested core sales, pricing, and calendar data from the Kaggle M5 Case Competition into BigQuery as external tables, enabling scalable and efficient querying.
  • Identified and integrated supplementary data sources to enrich the solution:
    • Imported economic indicators (such as gas prices, CPI, and unemployment rates) from the US Bureau of Labor Statistics into BigQuery using external tables.
    • Accessed weather data directly from BigQuery Public Datasets, allowing seamless integration.

Key Insights

  • Sales Intermittency: Many SKUs exhibit highly intermittent sales patterns, with frequent zero-sales periods and occasional spikes.
  • Stockouts and Holidays: Stockout events and major holidays are primary drivers of demand volatility.
  • External Drivers: Weather and economic indicators show measurable, category-specific impacts on sales, especially for food and seasonal items.
  • Feature Importance: Calendar features (holidays, SNAP), recent sales history, and price changes are among the most predictive variables.
  • Distribution: After log transformation, most SKUsâ€™ sales distributions approximate normality, improving model performance.
